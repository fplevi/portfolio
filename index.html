CASSI In production

BRL 4.5 million already recovered

Senior Product Designer

A self-service debt renegotiation platform that lets participants settle debts autonomously — no phone calls, no account required.

R$54.1M In installments
10k+ Negotiations
3k+ Reactivated contracts
Context

CASSI needed a digital channel for debt regularization of active and former participants. Before the project, interactions were mostly by phone without standardized processes — low autonomy, limited control.

Key challenges
  • Automate collection and communication processes
  • Simple, guided journey for both participants and CASSI operators
  • Secure access without traditional login for canceled plan holders
  • Enable outsourced negotiation flows through the platform
Solution highlights
  • Linear flow with 5-minute average completion time
  • Two-factor authentication — secure without account creation
  • Unified flow for all profiles (participants + operators)
  • PIX, bank slip, and account debit payment options
  • Real-time management dashboard with reports and exports
My contributions

User flow mapping, wireframing, high-fidelity Figma prototypes, requirement definition with commercial strategy and IT teams, legal validation, and back-office dashboard design.

Results — Jan 2025
  • BRL 54.1 million in installment negotiations
  • BRL 4.5 million paid through self-service
  • 3,000+ contracts reactivated with waiting period benefits
  • 10,000+ negotiations completed autonomously

CASSI In production

More subscriptions to health plans

Senior Product Designer

Redesigned the health plan enrollment journey — from a long, call-center-dependent flow to a fully digital, self-service experience.

3× More proposals in 1 year
408 Jan 2023 proposals
1,105 Jan 2024 proposals
Main problems identified
  • Mandatory pre-registration before users could see plans or prices
  • Too many form fields — errors, rework, high abandonment
  • Long, confusing steps with strong call center dependence
  • No visibility into proposal status or next steps
Solution highlights
  • Removed initial pre-registration — lower entry barrier
  • Flow reorganized to match the user's mental model: understand → simulate → choose → fill → submit → track
  • Clearer simulator with better plan and pricing information
  • Two-factor auth for proposal validation via registration data
  • Structured status messages and notifications throughout
  • Reviewed transactional email flows for content and timing
My contributions

Led discovery including journey mapping and user interviews, defined final flows with IT and the call center, built high-fidelity Figma prototypes with variables for multi-scenario testing, and handled full UX-to-UI handoff.

Impact

Launched March 2023. Web portal proposals nearly tripled in one year (408 → 1,105). Redesigned flow and learnings were reused for plan changes and contract retention.

CASSI In development

Scaling with design system

Leadership & Technical Performance

Conceived and built Harmony — consolidating 5 separate product libraries into a single source of truth for design, code, and content.

50% Faster cycle time
16→8wk Delivery reduction
Jan/26 Storybook launched
The problem

Five separate Figma libraries across different products made maintenance complex and slow. UI decisions weren't propagating, teams mixed components from different contexts, and cross-channel inconsistency grew with every new product.

Key architectural decisions
  • React + Next.js — market-dominant stack broadening the talent pool and enabling AI-assisted development
  • Adobe React Aria — built-in accessibility primitives remove solved problems from the roadmap
  • ContentOps integration — tone of voice and microcopy guidelines linked directly to component choices
  • Equity-driven design — accessibility as a structural requirement, not an afterthought
What Harmony unifies
  • Design tokens: color, typography, spacing, motion
  • Component library — web, intranet, and apps
  • Accessibility baselines (WCAG AA by default)
  • ContentOps: tone of voice and microcopy guidelines
  • Layout patterns and documentation
Results

First product on Harmony: 16 weeks from discovery to production. Second product, same conditions: 8 weeks — 50% reduction. Cross-channel consistency is now structural, not dependent on individual discipline.

My contributions

3 years of advocacy to get it prioritized, initial articulation and governance definition, hands-on component and token building, and cross-product review and alignment.
